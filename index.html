Controls

Model: each call's latency is a mix of a fast mode N(80, 8) ms and a heavy tail mode N(slow, slow/10) ms, with the tail hit at rate w = 1 − reliability. Parallel waits for the slowest call (max): the tail mode takes over as N grows. Serial sums the chain: the whole distribution marches right by the mean, and the average alone blows the budget. Independence is the optimistic case, not the safe one—correlated stalls (a shared backend, a GC pause) only move it the wrong way. The numbers are validated against model.py (P(bad)=1−(1−w)N for parallel). All in-browser, no network. This bimodal experiment follows Marc Brooker's Tail Latency Might Matter More Than You Think (2021); the effect was named by Dean & Barroso, The Tail at Scale (2013).
